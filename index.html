Andorra

Andorra

Proportion:7:10
Year adopted:1971

Symbolism:

The design is related to the flags of France, Spain, Catalonia and Foix, the lands historically linked with Andorra.
A flag of three stripes is similar to that of the French Tricolor, while the pattern of a wider middle stripe can be noted on the Spanish flag.
The blue and red of the Andorran flag are also found on the French flag, with red and yellow also being the colors of the Catalan flag (as the old royal symbol of the Crown of Aragon) and the arms of the old County of Foix (currently part of France).
The coat of arms of Andorra in the center of the flag contains historical symbolism to Bishop of Urgell, Count of Foix, Catalonia, and the Viscounty of Béarn.

Fun facts:

Although the three vertical stripes may at first appear to be of equal width, the centre yellow stripe is slightly wider than the other two so that the ratio of stripe widths is 8:9:8.
The design is similar to the flags of Romania, Moldova, and Chad but these have stripes of equal width.
The coat of arms makes it one of 28 national flags to contain Christian symbolism.

Belgium

Belgium

Proportion:13:15
Year adopted:1831

Symbolism:

The colours were taken from the coat of arms of the Duchy of Brabant, a gold lion with red claws and tongue on a black field.
The vertical design is probably based on the flag of France.

Fun facts:

In August 1830, the day after the rioting at the Brussels Opera and the start of the Belgian Revolution, the flag of France flew from the city hall of Brussels. The insurgents replaced it with a tricolour of red, yellow, and black horizontal stripes (similar to the one used during the Brabant Revolution of 1789-1790 which had established the United States of Belgium) made at a nearby fabric store. As a result, the Constitution of Belgium describes the colours of the Belgian nation as Red, Yellow, and Black, the reverse order shown in the official flag.
In January 1831, the stripes changed from horizontal to vertical, and in October, the flag attained its modern form, with the black placed at the hoist side of the flag.
